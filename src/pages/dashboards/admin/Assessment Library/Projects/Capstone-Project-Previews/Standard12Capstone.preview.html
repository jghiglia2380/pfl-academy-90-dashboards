Project Scenarios

Scenario 1: Understanding Game Odds

Your friend is curious about how gambling games work and has asked you to help explain the math behind them. Using what you've learned about probability, let's analyze some common games to understand why "the house always wins."

Key Probability Concepts
  • Probability = Number of favorable outcomes ÷ Total number of possible outcomes
  • Expected Value = (Probability × Win Amount) - (Probability of Loss × Loss Amount)
  • Example: Flipping a coin
    • Probability of heads = 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5 or 50%
    • If you win $2 on heads and lose $1 on tails:
    • Expected Value = (0.5 × $2) - (0.5 × $1) = $1 - $0.50 = $0.50

Game Examples

Simple Dice Game
  • Roll two dice
  • Win $5 if you roll a 7
  • Win $2 if you roll an 11
  • Costs $1 to play
  • Example calculation:
    • Ways to roll a 7: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1) = 6 ways
    • Total possible outcomes: 6 × 6 = 36
    • Probability of rolling 7 = 6/36 = 1/6
Coin Flip Game
  • Flip three coins
  • Win $8 if all three are heads
  • Win $1 if two are heads
  • Costs $1 to play
  • Example calculation:
    • Probability of all heads = 1/2 × 1/2 × 1/2 = 1/8
    • Ways to get exactly two heads: (H,H,T), (H,T,H), (T,H,H) = 3 ways
    • Probability of two heads = 3/8
Number Guessing Game
  • Pick a number between 1-10
  • Win $9 if you guess correctly
  • Costs $1 to play
  • Example calculation:
    • Only one correct number out of ten possible numbers
    • Probability = 1/10 = 0.10 or 10%
    • Expected Value = (0.1 × $9) - (0.9 × $1)

Your Analysis:

  1. For each game:
    • List all possible outcomes
    • Count favorable outcomes
    • Calculate probability of winning
    • Calculate expected value per play
  2. If you played 100 times, how much would you expect to win or lose?
  3. Which game gives you the best odds? Why?
  4. Explain why these games favor the house

Scenario 2: State Lottery

You've probably seen lottery tickets being sold at stores. Let's look at how the lottery works and why it's important to understand the odds before spending money on tickets.

Lottery Game Details

How It Works
  • Pick 3 numbers (0-9)
  • Each ticket costs $1
  • Win $500 if you match all numbers in order
  • Win $80 if you match all numbers in any order
  • Win $10 if you match two numbers
Example Spending
  • Someone spends $5 every week on tickets
  • That's about $260 per year
  • Could buy:
    • A new gaming console
    • Several months of streaming services
    • New clothes or shoes

Your Analysis:

  1. What are the odds of winning each prize?
  2. How much would someone expect to lose in a year?
  3. What else could they do with that money?
  4. Why do you think people still play despite the odds?

Scenario 3: Making Smart Choices

Your friend Sara just got her first part-time job making $12 per hour, working 15 hours per week. She's excited about having her own money and is thinking about buying some lottery tickets or trying some gambling games online. Help her make smart decisions about her money.

Sara's Situation

Monthly Money
  • Income: About $720 per month
  • Wants to:
    • Save for a car ($5000 goal)
    • Have money for food and movies with friends
    • Start saving for college
    • Buy some new clothes
Gambling Interest
  • Thinking of spending $20/week on games
  • Saw someone win $500 online
  • Friends say it's an easy way to make money

Help Sara Make a Plan:

  1. Calculate how long it would take to save for the car:
    • Without gambling
    • If spending $20/week on games
  2. List better ways to use the $20/week
  3. Explain the risks of gambling to Sara
  4. Suggest fun alternatives within her budget
